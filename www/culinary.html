Kuala Lumpur

Kuala Lumpur’s culinary scene is a melting pot of cultures, blending Malay, Chinese, Indian, and other influences into a rich food culture that truly represents the heart of Malaysia. This diversity makes it a paradise for food lovers, with vibrant street food, authentic local eateries, and upscale restaurants. Here’s a guide to some must-try dishes and where to find them:

# Food Location
1 Nasi Lemak Village Park Restaurant, Damansara Uptown.
2 Char Kway Teow Robert Char Kway Teow, Petaling Jaya.
3 Bak Kut Teh Restoran Sun Fong Bak Kut Teh, Jalan Raja Laut

Penang

Penang is celebrated as Malaysia's food paradise, blending Chinese, Malay, and Indian influences into a vibrant culinary tapestry. With street food and hawker stalls at its core, the state’s cuisine is all about bold flavors and aromatic spices, making it a must-visit for food lovers.

# Food Location
1 Penang Asam Laksa Pasar Air Itam Laksa, Kek Lok Si Temple in Air Itam.
2 Penang Hokkien Mee 888 Hokkien Mee, Presgrave Street Hawker Centre.
3 Rojak Pasembur Padang Kota Lama (Esplanade) hawker center.

Kedah

Kedah known as the “Rice Bowl of Malaysia,” is rooted in traditional Malay cuisine with fresh, homegrown ingredients. Dishes here are simpler and earthier than in Penang, reflecting the state’s agricultural heritage and influence from neighboring Thailand..

# Food Location
1 Nasi Ulam Restoran Zakaria Laksa Teluk Kechai, Alor Setar.
2 Pulut Durian Restoran Nasi Lemak Royale, Alor Setar.
3 Gulai Tempoyak Ikan Patin Restoran Kak Nor Gulai Tempoyak, Jitra.
